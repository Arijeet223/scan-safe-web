QR Payment Frauds

How scammers steal your money through fraudulent QR codes

What are QR Payment Frauds?

QR payment frauds involve scammers using counterfeit QR codes to trick people into sending money directly to the scammer's account instead of legitimate merchants. These scams exploit the convenience and growing popularity of QR-based payment systems.

In India, QR payment scams have resulted in losses of over ₹27 crore in the past year alone, with an average loss of ₹9,800 per victim.

How QR Payment Frauds Work

1

Replacing Legitimate QR Codes

Scammers place their own QR codes over legitimate ones at shops, restaurants, or payment kiosks.

2

Creating Fake Payment Requests

Instead of receiving money, scanning these codes often initiates a payment to the scammer's account.

3

Money Transfer

Once payment is made, the money is quickly moved through multiple accounts, making recovery difficult.

Common QR Payment Scam Scenarios

Fake Payment Collection

Scammers pose as businesses collecting payments and provide their own QR codes, often offering "special discounts" to entice victims.

Fraudulent Cashback Offers

Scammers promote fake cashback schemes requiring you to scan a QR code that actually initiates a payment from your account.

Tampered Merchant Codes

Physical QR codes at legitimate businesses are replaced or covered with fraudulent ones that route payments to scammers.

Case Studies

Mumbai Restaurant Scam (2023)

Fraudsters replaced QR codes at over 25 restaurants in Mumbai, collecting approximately ₹8.3 lakh from unsuspecting customers before being detected.

Fake Electricity Bill Scam (2022)

Scammers sent fake electricity bill payment reminders with QR codes via SMS, resulting in over 300 victims losing money across Delhi-NCR.

E-commerce Refund Scam (2023)

Scammers called victims claiming to be from popular e-commerce platforms, offering refunds via QR code scans that actually drained accounts.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Verify that you're scanning a receive payment code, not a send payment code
  • Double-check the merchant name in your payment app before confirming
  • Be suspicious of unexpected cashback or discount offers requiring QR scans
  • Look for signs of tampering on physical QR codes (stickers placed over original codes)
  • When possible, use other payment methods if a QR code seems suspicious
  • Enable payment limits and transaction notifications on your payment apps

Been a Victim of QR Payment Fraud?

Report it immediately to your bank and the cybercrime portal. Quick action may help recover your funds.
