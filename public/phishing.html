QR Phishing Attacks

How scammers use QR codes to steal your personal information

What are QR Phishing Attacks?

QR Phishing (or "Quishing") is a technique where cybercriminals use malicious QR codes to direct victims to fake websites designed to steal login credentials, personal information, or financial details.

In 2023, QR phishing attacks increased by 743% compared to the previous year.

How QR Phishing Works

1

Placing Fake QR Codes

Scammers place counterfeit QR codes in public places, over legitimate business codes, or send them through email/messages.

2

Redirecting to Fake Websites

When scanned, these QR codes send users to spoofed websites that look identical to legitimate services.

3

Data Theft

When you enter your information on these fake sites, scammers capture your credentials, bank details, or personal information.

Real-World Examples

Fake Parking Payment Codes

Scammers place counterfeit QR codes on parking meters, directing people to fake payment sites that steal credit card information.

Banking Impersonation

Emails claiming to be from your bank include QR codes that lead to phishing sites designed to steal your banking credentials.

Restaurant Menu Scams

Fake QR codes placed on restaurant tables or windows claiming to show menus but instead lead to malicious websites.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use QR scanner apps with built-in security features that preview URLs
  • Always check the URL before proceeding to the website
  • Verify the URL matches the expected business domain
  • Never enter personal information on websites accessed through unexpected QR codes
  • Be wary of QR codes in public places or unsolicited communications
