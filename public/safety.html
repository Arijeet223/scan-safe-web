QR Safety Guide

Essential tips to protect yourself from QR code scams

With QR code usage increasing by 96% in the past two years, scammers are exploiting this technology to steal information and money. Learn how to scan safely with these essential precautions.

Before You Scan

Verify the Source

Only scan QR codes from trusted sources. Be especially careful of codes in unexpected places or from unknown senders.

Example: A QR code randomly posted on a street pole should be treated with extreme suspicion.

Inspect the Physical QR Code

Check for signs of tampering, such as stickers placed over original codes or suspicious looking printouts.

Example: If a QR code at a restaurant table looks like it was added later or covers another code, it could be fraudulent.

Use a Secure QR Scanner

Use QR scanners from reputable developers that offer preview features to see the URL before visiting.

Example: Some scanner apps like Kaspersky's QR Scanner include security checks that alert you to suspicious links.

While Scanning

Preview URLs Before Visiting

Always check the destination URL before proceeding. Look for misspellings, unusual domains, or unexpected URLs.

Example: If you're scanning what you think is a restaurant menu but the URL is "bit.ly/2x3y4z" instead of the restaurant's domain, be cautious.

Beware of Automatic Downloads

Be suspicious if scanning a QR code immediately triggers a download without your consent.

Example: If a QR code starts downloading a file called "menu.apk" instead of showing a website, cancel immediately.

Check for HTTPS

Ensure websites use HTTPS (look for a lock icon). Avoid entering personal information on HTTP sites.

Example: A legitimate banking website will always use https:// in their URL, not http://.

For Financial Transactions

Verify Payment Direction

Confirm whether you're scanning to pay or to receive money. Many scams involve "receive payment" QR codes that actually send money.

Example: When a street vendor asks you to scan their QR code to pay, double-check that you're not actually receiving a payment request.

Confirm Merchant Details

Check that the merchant name displayed in your payment app matches the business you're paying.

Example: If you're at "ABC Restaurant" but your payment app shows "XYZ Enterprises," the QR code may be fraudulent.

General Digital Hygiene

Use Security Software

Install and keep updated reputable security software on your mobile devices.

Example: Applications like Avast Mobile Security can help detect and block malicious QR code destinations.

Keep Your Device Updated

Regularly update your device's operating system and apps to patch security vulnerabilities.

Example: Many malware attacks exploit known vulnerabilities that have already been fixed in newer software versions.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Activate 2FA on financial and sensitive accounts to add an extra layer of security.

Example: Even if scammers get your login credentials through a QR phishing attack, 2FA can prevent account access.

Red Flags: When to Avoid Scanning

Unexpected QR Codes

Be wary of QR codes in unsolicited emails, messages, or unexpected locations.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers

Extreme discounts, massive cashbacks, or unexpected prizes requiring QR scanning are often scams.

Sense of Urgency

QR codes accompanied by messages creating artificial time pressure ("Scan now before offer expires!").

Requests for Sensitive Information

Any QR code that leads to a site asking for your personal details, banking information, or passwords.

Poor Grammar or Spelling

Scan destinations with unprofessional appearance, poor translation, or spelling mistakes.

Your QR Safety Checklist

If You've Been Scammed

1

Act Immediately

Contact your bank or payment provider to report unauthorized transactions and potentially freeze your account.

2

File a Cybercrime Report

Report the incident on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in.

3

Document Everything

Take screenshots, save emails, and document all details of the scam including the QR code if possible.

4

Scan Your Device

Run a comprehensive security scan to check for malware or persistent threats.

5

Change Your Passwords

Update passwords for all important accounts, especially financial services.
